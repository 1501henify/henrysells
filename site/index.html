Day 45, Henry Sells Development

Development Notes & Limitations

While building Henry Sells as a solo developer, I had to rethink how trust is built on e‑commerce platforms. Established marketplaces like Amazon rely on verified accounts, carts, and automated checkout. In contrast, my stack (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Firebase, GitHub, Vercel) meant I had to implement a lightweight alternative.

I used a direct‑to‑chat approach—redirecting buyers to business WhatsApp or Telegram with their selected products. This keeps things personal, transparent, and simple.

However, I discovered a major limitation: platforms like wa.me and Telegram links do not send messages instantly. Instead, they place the product details in a text box, requiring the user to press "Send" manually. This gives room for altering prices or messages, which reduces professionalism.

I'm exploring ways to send fixed, uneditable product summaries (images + prices) without changing my existing stack.

If you have ideas or experience with this problem, please share them below — it really helps.
